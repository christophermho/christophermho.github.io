TD Lab Internship
  • Role • Product Design Intern
  • Team • Designers: Christopher H, Developers: Omas A, Jacob G, Sophie Q
  • Date • September to December 2018
  • Tools • Sketch, InVision, Zeplin, SurveyMonkey
Context
TD Lab is an innovation workspace within TD Bank, exploring emerging tech & customer experiences. During my internship, I led the design of a mobile app, N2C (Newcomer to Canada) that helps Canadian newcomers settle in by teaching them about cultural differences between their home country and Canada.
Problem
TD’s Everyday Banking team presented our team with the opportunity statement below:
“Canada is expected to welcome 1 million new permanent residents over the next 3 years. How might TD Bank develop a new digital experience to incentivize these residents to make TD Bank their bank of choice?”
Goal
Canadian Newcomers
•   To have a stress-free immigration process, including opening a Canadian banking account
TD Bank
•   To ease Canadian newcomers’ immigration process and gain more customers (i.e. open more bank accounts)
To scope the project, we held a design sprint. We researched existing competition, selected primary users, mapped their journey, and brainstormed potential solutions by sketching wireframes and disccusing as a team.
Competitive Analysis
To gain a better understanding of the types of products and services banks were offering to Canadian newcomers, we looked at the websites of the Big Five Banks (TD, RBC, Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC).
Key Insights
•   Every bank offered some type of newcomer package with perks for opening an account with them (i.e. no monthly fees and/or international transfer fees, cash bonuses, special credit cards, etc.
•   A few banks also provided checklists for pre-arrival, just arrived, and setting in
Primary Users
Indian newcomers were designated as the primary users and were chosen for the following reasons:
•   They represent 12% of all recent immigration to Canada (147,000+ new residents)
•   They are the youngest on average and have the highest amount of immigrants in the 25—35 age range, characteristics of a population that are beginning their banking journey
•   They place a heavy focus on large financial obligations, like buying/leasing a vehicle or the arrival of a child
•   English is a co-official language in India

Initial Design
The initial design was a mobile app that took users step-by-step through the immigration process. Similar to a guide, the app would assist users with tasks like completing important documents, estimating expenses, booking flights, etc. Also, a TD 'E-card' would be offered to the user. Users would be able to earn points by completing steps within the app and could redeem their points for a monetary value by opening a Canadian TD bank account.
User Interviews + Project Redefinition
While the initial design seemed useful, we couldn’t be sure without actually consulting the users. We also thought the design seemed too similar to existing competition (i.e. an immigration checklist, which TD Bank already offers). To make sure we were correctly approaching the problem, I conducted user interviews with recent Canadian immigrants to truly understand the problems they faced during their immigration process.
Key Insights
•   If a user needed information about the Canadian immigration process, they would simply do a quick google search
•   The most trustworthy sources were Canadian government websites
•   Lorem ipsum
In other words, there was little reason for users to download an app to find information that was more accessible and trustworthy, through a couple of clicks. Also, providing immigration information could pose liability problems if it was not constantly accurate and updated.
With these insights, we decided to redefine the project. We wanted to create a product that was more casual, but still provided relevant and useful information, effectively differentiating itself from the current competition.
New Design
Enter N2C (Newcomer to Canada), a mobile app that teaches users about the cultural differences between their home country and Canada. Using both the user’s country of origin and their current location, N2C creates a customized experience by providing tailored information for the user. Like the first iteration, N2C would have a rewards system to earn points that could be redeemed for money by opening a TD account.
Everyday, newcomers experience different pain points related to niche things they haven't learned about, like for example, "How much do I tip? What even is tipping?" N2C is filled with cultural differences like these, ranging over many topics. The purpose of N2C is to alleviate these daily difficulties, making users' move to Canada easy and welcoming. It’s easy to google, “What immigration forms do I need to complete?”, but many users don’t know what they don’t know and that isn't easy to search for.
Usability Testing
After designing mockups and creating a prototype, it was time to validate the idea. I tested N2C with six users, all Indian immigrants who permanently moved to Canada within the last five years.
Key Insights
•   The most important topics to users in order were: Laws, Banking, and Housing
•   The least important topics to users were: Entertainment and Sports
•   Users wanted reccomendations from the app (ex. suggesting nearby places of worship)
Overall, user feedback of the app was positive, with several users mentioning that if N2C existed, they would have downloaded it when they first came to Canada. The majority of users also thought that being able to earn and redeem points for money was a great incentive to invest time into the app, and eventually open a TD bank account. Users mentioned many personal anecdotes regarding specific things that they did not know about when they first came to Canada, so we knew the concept of N2C showed real value.
Final Design Changes
Incorporating feedback from the usability testing interviews, I implemented design changes in the next and final iteration. Here’s a highlight of what was added:
•   Profile
•   Onboarding and authentication
•   A complete rewards system (i.e. more ways to earn points/money)
